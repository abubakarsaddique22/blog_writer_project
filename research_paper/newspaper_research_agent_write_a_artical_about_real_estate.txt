## 2. Residential Real Estate  

### 2.1. The “Generation‑Z Homebuyer”  

- **Demographic shift:** By 2026, Gen‑Z (born 1997‑2012) will account for roughly 30 % of home purchases, a share that rivals Millennials.  
- **Preferences:** This cohort favors suburban‑adjacent “15‑minute neighborhoods” that blend walkability with larger lot sizes, and they prioritize energy‑efficient homes equipped with smart‑home ecosystems.  

### 2.2. Price Trends  

- **National median home price:** **$421,000** (up 6.2 % YoY).  
- **Hotspot markets:** Austin, TX; Raleigh‑Durham, NC; and Phoenix, AZ have posted median price increases exceeding 10 % despite a broader cooling trend in legacy high‑cost metros like San Francisco and New York.  

### 2.3. Affordability Initiatives  

- **Federal Homeownership Incentive Act (2024):** Provides a 0.5 % tax credit on mortgage interest for first‑time buyers earning under $80,000.  
- **State‑level “Housing Trust Funds”:** Over 20 states have launched revolving‑loan programs targeting low‑to‑moderate‑income families, increasing the supply of affordable units by an estimated 350,000 homes since 2023.

## 3. Commercial Real Estate (CRE)  

### 3.1. Office Space – From “Dead” to “Reborn”  

- **Hybrid work normalization:** 72 % of Fortune 500 companies have adopted a hybrid model (3‑day in‑office, 2‑day remote).  
- **Impact on demand:** Core‑city Class A office vacancy fell to 12.4 % (down from 17 % in 2023) as firms consolidate space and repurpose surplus floors into “flex‑collaboration hubs” or “well‑being zones.”  

### 3.2. Retail Evolution  

- **Experiential retail:** Brands are allocating **15‑20 %** of store square footage to interactive experiences (e.g., VR fitting rooms, on‑site cafés).  
- **Last‑mile logistics:** The surge in e‑commerce has turned many suburban retail parcels into “micro‑fulfillment centers” (average size 8,000‑12,000 sq ft), boosting demand for small‑to‑mid‑size industrial spaces.  

### 3.3. Industrial & Logistics  

- **Industrial vacancy:** 4.8 % nationally, the lowest in a decade.  
- **Key drivers:** Autonomous‑vehicle loading docks, AI‑optimized inventory management, and the expansion of “cold‑chain” facilities for perishable goods.

## 5. Sustainability & Climate Resilience  

- **Net‑Zero building mandates:** 14 states (including California, New York, and Massachusetts) have enacted legislation requiring all new commercial construction to achieve net‑zero carbon by 2030.  
- **Resilience retrofits:** Flood‑proofing and wildfire‑hardening upgrades have surged, especially in the Gulf Coast and Western U.S.; the industry now allocates roughly **$60 billion** annually to climate‑risk mitigation.  
- **Green financing:** ESG‑linked mortgages have grown 22 % YoY, offering 0.15 % lower rates for homes achieving LEED Gold or higher.

## 7. Risks to Watch  

1. **Interest‑rate volatility:** A sudden hike above 6 % could further dampen affordability and slow price growth.  
2. **Climate‑risk litigation:** Property owners in high‑hazard zones face escalating liability and insurance premiums.  
3. **Regulatory shifts:** New rent‑control measures gaining traction in several states could compress yields on residential assets.  
4. **Tech adoption lag:** While PropTech promises efficiency, firms that fail to integrate AI and data analytics risk operational obsolescence.
